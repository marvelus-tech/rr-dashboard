---

### 2. Market Demand

**Freeze Risk Profile:**
- **Average Winter Low:** 21°F (January 30) | Daily lows around 26°F | WeatherSpark
- **Freeze Days:** ~60-80 days below 32°F per year
- **Deep Freeze Risk:** Pipes freeze when temps stay below 20°F for 6+ hours — happens multiple times per winter in Indiana
- **Burst Pipe Risk:** HIGH. Indiana winters regularly cause frozen/burst pipes. Local plumbing companies advertise "frozen pipe repair" as a core service
- **Statewide Issue:** Indiana Office of Utility Consumer Counselor publishes "Preventing Frozen Pipes" guides — this is a known, recurring problem

**Search Volume Estimates:**
- "Burst pipe repair Carmel IN" — ~10-25/month baseline
- "Frozen pipe repair Carmel" — ~15-30/month
- "Emergency plumber Carmel" — ~50-100/month
- **Spike potential:** 5x-10x during cold snaps (Jan-Feb)
- **Seasonality:** Oct-Apr (winter season), with spikes during polar vortex events

**Demand Drivers:**
- Winter cold snaps (Dec-Mar)
- Polar vortex events (increasing frequency)
- Aging housing stock (33 years old) — plumbing aging out
- New construction with PEX plumbing (less burst risk) BUT Carmel has many older homes
- Affluent homeowners = call plumber immediately, don't DIY

---

### 4. Industry Fit

**VERDICT: EXCELLENT FIT** ✅✅

**Pros:**
- Carmel winters are REAL — pipes freeze, pipes burst. This is not theoretical demand.
- Affluent homeowners ($134K median income) = call professionals immediately
- 74% homeownership = homeowners with insurance, not renters waiting for landlords
- High home values ($570K) = homeowners protect their investment
- No franchise dominance = easier to rank
- 33-year-old housing stock = aging copper plumbing reaching failure age
- Indianapolis suburb = enough population density without metro competition

**Cons:**
- Newer homes (built 1992+) may have PEX plumbing = less burst risk
- Carmel is a wealthy suburb = may have newer infrastructure than Indianapolis proper
- Winter is seasonal = demand drops May-September
- Job values may be lower than $6K estimate (more like $1,500-3,000 for burst pipe repair)

**The Real Question:** Is burst pipe repair the RIGHT niche for Carmel?

**Answer:** YES, but the job value estimate is inflated. Burst pipe repair is typically $1,000-3,000, not $6,000. The $6K estimate may be conflating burst pipe repair with water damage restoration. However, burst pipe repair often LEADS to water damage restoration, so the total job value can be $5K-10K when including remediation.

**Better framing:** "Emergency plumbing + water damage restoration" as a combined service. This captures the burst pipe call AND the follow-up restoration work.

**Recommendation:** **KEEP Carmel.** It's a strong market. But consider expanding the niche to "emergency plumbing" or "water damage restoration" to capture more leads and higher job values.
