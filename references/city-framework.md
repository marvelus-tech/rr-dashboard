# City Selection Framework

## Target City Criteria

### Ideal Population Range
- **50,000 - 500,000** (mid-size cities)
- Avoid: Major metros >1M (too competitive)
- Avoid: Tiny towns <10K (not enough search volume)

### City Types (Ranked by Opportunity)

| Rank | City Type | Why | Examples |
|------|-----------|-----|----------|
| 1 | **Suburbs of major metros** | Access to metro demand, less competition | Fort Worth (Dallas), Mesa (Phoenix), Aurora (Denver) |
| 2 | **Standalone mid-size cities** | Self-contained market, moderate competition | Wichita KS, Des Moines IA, Boise ID |
| 3 | **Satellite cities** | Commuter towns, growing population | Frisco TX, Lehi UT, Meridian ID |
| 4 | **State capitals** | Government + private sector | Topeka KS, Lincoln NE, Helena MT |
| 5 | **College towns** | Student + permanent population | Bloomington IN, Ames IA, Logan UT |
| 6 | **Retirement destinations** | Aging population = service demand | The Villages FL, Prescott AZ, Bend OR |
| 7 | **Boom towns** | Rapid growth, infrastructure lag | Austin suburbs, Raleigh suburbs |

### Avoid These
- Major metros: NYC, LA, Chicago, Houston, Phoenix, Philadelphia, San Antonio, San Diego, Dallas, San Jose
- Saturated suburbs: Where major marketing companies already dominate
- Cities with strong franchise presence (Servpro, Stanley Steemer, etc. dominating)

## Research Methodology

### Step 1: Identify Metro Areas
List top 50 US metro areas by population. Target their suburbs.

### Step 2: Check Google Map Pack
For each niche+city combo:
1. Search `"[service] [city]"`
2. Check Map Pack (top 3 local businesses)
3. Red flags:
   - 3+ businesses with 50+ reviews each
   - Major franchises (SERVPRO, Stanley Steemer, etc.)
   - Businesses with active GMB posts/photos
4. Green flags:
   - 0-2 GMB listings
   - Weak websites (2005-era design)
   - No major franchise presence

### Step 3: Check Organic Results
1. Are top 10 results dominated by major directories (Angi, HomeAdvisor)?
2. Are there local business sites ranking?
3. Do local sites look outdated/weak?
4. Is there room for a new, optimized site?

### Step 4: Verify Search Volume
Target: 20-100 monthly searches
- Too low (<10): Not enough leads
- Too high (500+): Too competitive

### Step 5: Domain Availability Check
Look for domains including:
- `[service][city].com`
- `[city][service].com`
- `[service]-[city].com`
- `[city]-[service].com`

## Quick Screening Questions

For any city, ask:
1. Is population between 50K-500K?
2. Are there <3 strong GMB listings for this niche?
3. Do existing sites look outdated?
4. Is there no major franchise dominance?
5. Is search volume 20-100/month?
6. Can I buy a relevant domain?

If 4+ are YES → Green light. Build it.
If 2-3 are YES → Yellow. Research further.
If <2 are YES → Red. Move on.
